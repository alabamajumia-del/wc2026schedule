## 1. Google Trends Interpretation Rules

Google Trends normalizes search interest on a relative 0-100 scale for the selected region and time range. A score of 100 means peak relative interest in that comparison, not absolute search volume.

For related queries:

- Top queries show the most searched related queries in the selected scope.
- Rising queries show terms with the largest growth during the selected time range.
- Very large rising percentages usually mean the query had little prior search activity and is now emerging.

## 9. Recommended Keyword Density Adjustment

The original project rule says:

```text
Primary exact match: 1%-2%
Keyword family density: 3%-5%
```

For the main schedule tool page, the practical target should be adjusted:

```text
Primary exact match: 0.5%-1.0%
Keyword family density: 1.5%-3.0%
```

Reason:

```text
The page contains a large fixture table, team names, cities, stadiums and interactive UI labels. Forcing a 3%-5% keyword-family density across the full visible page would encourage low-quality repetition.
```

The PDF and dates pages can keep the original density target more easily because they have narrower article-style content.
